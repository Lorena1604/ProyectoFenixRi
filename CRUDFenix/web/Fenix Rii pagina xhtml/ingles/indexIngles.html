What is Taekwondo ?

Taekwondo is a martial art transformed into Olympic combat sport since 1988 , when it was introduced as a demonstration sport at the Olympic Games held in the city of Seoul in South Korea. The meaning of this is the philosophy of fist and kick ...

TAE: Foot, kick.

KWON: Fist, blocking.

DO: Path, art, method or philosophy of life.

Therefore , the word " taekwondo " could be translated as "the way of fist and kick ", meaning it is a martial art that uses only the feet, the Hands and others Body ( such as: the knees and elbows ), ignoring completely the use of arms , of both traditional and modern.

Taekwondo Colombia History

Taekwondo came to Colombia in 1964 and started operating in the cities of Bogota and Medellin by Korean professor Sahn young Han. In Medellin was practiced in 1966 by the Korean Professor Sohn Su Huan ...

For 1967 it institutionalizes and formalizes taekwondo in Colombia, through Convention of the America University in Bogota, and the Government of Korea, along with Master Deuk Lee Kyong comes to the country.

Many other Korean teachers will influence Colombian taekwondo , most fleeting way , but somehow left their mark on the followers of different parts in the country.

General characteristics

Taekwondo was characterized by its extensive use of techniques leg and kicks, Which they are much more varied and have important role as martial arts. Also, the refined technique of The Same make them stand out for their great speed and accuracy...

The importance given to techniques fist and open hand, depends on the style ( ITF or WTF) practiced , Coach and School Where is practiced. Unfortunately today, many schools tend to neglect the training of techniques and Puno open hand , as their use is increasingly restricted in the competition , giving you more score to kicks. However , good training should include both hand techniques and foot techniques of Taekwondo , and should not be focused solely on the success in the competition, but the domain and knowledge of martial art.

The Dobok

It is the uniform used in the practice of Korean martial arts. Do means " the way of life ' and bok means ' workout clothes '

Dobok traditional colors are black and white , although in some martial arts like Hap Ki Do can be found ...

The kasaka can be opened by the center or closed , depending on the martial art that is practiced, and both the pants are usually slightly curved.
